Quantum information, sensing, and learning

I study how quantum systems can be represented, tested, learned, and used for sensing.

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Southern California advised by Prof. Quntao Zhuang. My work spans quantum error correction, bosonic encodings, quantum state and process tomography, variational quantum algorithms, quantum machine learning, and non-Gaussian entanglement.

Before USC, I completed an MSc in Physics at the University of Calgary with Prof. David Feder and Prof. Barry Sanders, and a BSc in Physics at the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences.

Research Interests

Quantum Learning

Variational and machine-learning-based quantum algorithms for extracting useful information from quantum systems.

Quantum Sensing

Bosonic variational sensor networks, physical-layer data learning, and entanglement-assisted sensing protocols.

Error Correction

Stabilizer states, graph-state representations, topological order, and quantum error-correction codes.

Tomography

Complexity and structure in quantum state and process tomography, including non-Gaussian resources.
